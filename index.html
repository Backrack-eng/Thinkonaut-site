What it does

A single place for thoughts you can speak, type, shape, and find again—without turning your mind into a filing chore.

Think out loud—or on the page

Record when you’re moving, write when you’re still. Same calm capture either way, so the idea lands before it drifts.

Turn raw input into structured notes

Voice or text becomes something you can read, edit, and search—clear enough to act on, whether you’re capturing a conversation or refining a line of thought.

Turn thoughts into structured lists

Quickly organize ideas into checkable, evolving lists without breaking your flow.

Build projects from fragments

Connect ideas across time so concepts, drafts, and directions stay aligned.

See relationships in Galaxy view

Your library becomes a constellation—affinities surface, projects hold their place, and distance reads as meaning, not clutter.

How it works

A quiet rhythm: catch the thought, give it shape, revisit it, connect it—then let Galaxy view show you the whole sky.

  1. 1

    Catch the thought

    Record it, or write it—half-formed is fine.

  2. 2

    Shape it into a note

    Structure you can edit, title, and search—lists when the thought needs steps.

  3. 3

    Return as it develops

    Shape the idea into something clear — a list, a set of notes, a direction, or the start of something bigger.

  4. 4

    Tie it to a project

    Gather what belongs together; the project grows as you add notes.

  5. 5

    See the whole field

    Galaxy view shows how ideas relate—clusters, bridges, and room to breathe.
